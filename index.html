Mt Rainier is an active volcano.

It has a history of catastrophic lahars.
---- A lahar is a fast-moving, powerful flowing mixture of water, rock, and mud that comes from the volcano - they are very dangerous.

Lahars are rare.
---- However, you, your family, and your wider community may be at risk if you live, work, or go to school in the Lahar Zone.

If a lahar happens and you are in a Lahar Zone, you may only have minutes to evacuate.

You have the power to protect yourself and your loved ones - prepare now!


Be Lahar Ready.


Follow these simple steps:

Be Aware.

Know Your Hazard. Go to the Lahars page to learn about lahars and where in the Puget Sound Lowlands they might impact.

Be Prepared.

Know how to be ready. Go to the Prepare page to learn about the best steps to take to make sure you are ready to respond in the event of a lahar.

Be Safe!

Know what to do. Go to the Evacuate page to learn about how to respond to a lahar, including which evacuation routes to take.
If you hear a siren or receive an alert, get out and get uphill -- fast!

You may only have minutes to evacuate.
