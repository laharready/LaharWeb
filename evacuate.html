Step 3: Be Safe - Beat it!

Know how to be safe.

Warning: Incoming Lahar!


In the event of a lahar, you will hear sirens, activated by the USGS lahar warning system, and if you have cell signal, you should receive a text alert on your phone. Unless it is announced, you should assume that it is not a drill. If you have signed up to additional alerts, you should receive a message on your phone.

Importantly, do not delay. Move fast.

Use the following drop down sections to learn more.

How to Evacuate (Individuals/Families).


Remember: there is no safe level of exposure to a lahar. You must evacuate and GET TO HIGH GROUND to avoid danger.

The best way to do this, if you are able-bodied, is to leave on foot.

This may feel counterintuitive, but just as you are thinking, 'I must leave fast, I'll take the car!', others are thinking (and doing) exactly the same. The consequence? Traffic jams -- these slow everyone down.

Getting out on foot is the most efficient and safest way for everyone in your family and wider community.

Take a look at the 'Evacuation Routes' section below to see where you should go.

Remembering the following phrase might help...


"
If there’s a lahar
---- forget the car!
Walk your feet off down the street
And get on up that hill…
"



Evacuation of Schools.


A lahar could happen at any time of day.

Because of this, schools are mandated by the State of Washington to prepare robust evacuation plans. These plans are designed to ensure that your children are safely evacuated in the event of a lahar.

People learn how to do something well by doing it many times. This is why schools are mandated to run lahar evacuation drills regularly. These are either shelter-in-place drills or full evacuation drills. It is so important for your child/children to know how to keep themselves safe in case of a lahar emergency
-- Please allow them to take part in drills.

For parents of children at schools in the Lahar Zone: For the sake of your safety, and that of your children, you should not collect your children from school in the event of a lahar. This goes against every parental instinct. However, staying in or entering the Lahar Zone, especially in your car, is dangerous. Stay safe so that you can greet your family once evacuations are over.



Evacuation Routes (by Location).

Evacuation routes have been carefully planned by local authorities to ensure that you can get out of the way of a lahar as quickly as possible. They are marked by Evacuation Signs like the one on the left. These will guide you out of the Lahar Zone and to a safe location.

In this section, you will find evacuation maps for each of the populated areas within Lahar Zone. Click/press the names to reveal details in the dropdown sections. You can save these maps to your computer or device.

Orting

Orting would be the first city in the path of a lahar - it could reach the city limits in about an hour.

Upstream of Orting, a lahar could come from the south along the Puyallup River, or from the north along the Carbon River and South Praire Creek. A lahar would approach Orting itself from the southeast.

Under the worst-case-scenario, the entire valley could be covered in up to 15 feet of mud and debris, - the whole of Orting would be affected. This means there is no safe place within the city limits and you must evacuate up the hills to the south west.

The maps above show the evacuation routes. Here is a description:

-- If you are in the main central part of Orting, your best option is to take Callistoga Avenue West towards the southwest, cross over the Puyallup River, and go past the Abundant Life Church. From here, either continue straight on along 162nd Avenue East up the hill, or turn right onto Ortin Kapaowskin Highway East up to the top of the hill.

-- If you are in the northwest of Orting, follow the 162 out of Orting, cross over the Puyallup River keep heading northwest until, either: 1) turn right onto McCutcheon Road East following it until Rhodes Lake Road East, onto which you can turn right and head up hill into Bonney Lake; 2) just beyond McCutcheon Road East, take a left onto Mililtary Road East and head up the hill.

-- If you are in the southeast of Orting follow the 162 southeast. You have two options. 1) Turn right when you reach Orville Road East to the south, you can then take left turns onto either Buttes Drive East or A. L. Card. Road East, both head up the hill to safety. 2) Keep going along the 162 to the northeast until you can turn right to join Patterson Road East which will take you up the hill to safety.
