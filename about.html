The Lahar Ready Project...

...is a collaboration between the Cascades Volcano Observatory of the United States Geological Survey (USGS), Pierce County Emergency Management, the East Pierce Interlocal Coallition for Emergency Management (EPIC), Washington State Emergency Management Division, and many of the school districts and communities in the Puget Sound Lowlands.

The goal of the Lahar Ready Project is to increase awareness of the hazard posed by lahars from Mount Rainier. We want to empower people living in the Puyallup, White, Carbon, and Nisqualy Valleys to help them develop plans to protect themselves in the event of a lahar by equipping them with the right information.

This website can be used to:
1) Learn about lahars: what they are and where they might affect.
2) Learn about how to prepare for a lahar.
3) Learn about how to evacuate in the event of a lahar.
